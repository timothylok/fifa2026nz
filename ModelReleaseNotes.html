02 — Motivation

Three Structural Issues Resolved

The original model exhibited three structural problems that this release addresses in full.

  • Overreaction to recent tournaments High K-values combined with a short decay half-life (~1.4 years) caused France, England, and Germany to collapse after single knockout losses, and over-rewarded teams on short hot streaks.
  • Inflated longshots Teams with low match volume — Morocco, Algeria, Japan — were over-rewarded for isolated high-K wins relative to their overall match history.
  • Underweighted favourites Raw Elo systematically undervalued top-tier teams due to qualifier noise, confederation imbalance, and no distinction between match types.
03 — Changes

Engineering Details

3.1 Decay Half-Life — Stability Fix

The Elo decay formula was changed from an exponential with a ~1.4-year effective half-life to a standard half-life parameterisation. A match from 4 years ago now retains 50% of its original weight, compared to 37% previously at 2 years.

k = K × 0.5 (delta_years / HALF_LIFE_YEARS) × (1 − λ)

HALF_LIFE_YEARS = 4    // was: exp(-t/2) ≈ 1.4yr half-life
~1.4 years → 4.0 years ~35% volatility reduction

3.2 Tournament Shrinkage — Volatility Control

A per-class shrinkage factor λ is applied to the Elo delta after the K-factor and decay. This reduces the magnitude of updates for high-pressure matches where luck and variance play an outsized role (penalty shootouts, knockout draws), without eliminating the signal entirely.

3.3 K-Factor Calibration — Bias Correction

A 700-combination grid search was run over four K-factor dimensions using match-level Brier scores on three held-out tournament windows. Match-level Brier was chosen over tournament-outcome Monte Carlo for statistical power (~165 data points vs 2–4 tournament winners).

Calibration targets: WC 2022 (weight 0.50), Euro 2020 (weight 0.25), Euro 2024 (weight 0.25). WC 2018 excluded — only 5 months of pre-tournament training data due to CSV starting January 2018.

The Brier surface was extremely flat across the search space (0.15953 → 0.15959), indicating a stable, well-conditioned model with no sharp minima or overfitting risk. The grid search confirms the chosen values rather than precisely selecting them.

3.4 Market Blend — Posterior Correction

Final championship probabilities remain a 60% market odds / 40% model blend, sourced from Polymarket. This corrects residual structural biases in raw Elo (confederation imbalance, low-volume team inflation) while preserving the model's predictive signal. Raw Elo is preserved separately as raw_win_pct in results.json.

4.2 Raw Elo (for transparency)

Raw Elo reflects pure performance signal without market correction. It is not a betting model — it measures relative team strength based on match history, decay, and shrinkage. Morocco's high raw Elo (8.1%) reflects genuine recent form; the market blend correctly weights it at 4.2% for public-facing output.
